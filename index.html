About Dancing with the Stars

What is Dancing with the Stars?

Dancing With the Stars is a competition reality TV show where celebrities are paired with professionally trained ballroom dancers and compete to win the Len Goodman Mirrorball Trophy. It first premiered on ABC in 2005 and now is streamed on Disney Plus or ABC every Tuesday in season. The show is hosted by former Mirrorball Trophy Champion Alfonso Riberio and former Pro and multi Mirrorball Trophy Champion Julianne Hough. The show is judged by Carrie Ann Inaba, Derek Hough, and Bruno Tonioli (all are professionally trained dancers).

Rules

  • Each week, couples are assigned a style of dance and a song. The professionally trained dancer (pro) is then in charge of cherographing a unique and captivating dance as well as responsible for teaching their celebrity partner how todo the dance.
  • The judges will then score on technique, style, difficulty, and performance. Each judge will give a score 1-10 resulting in a total out of 30. During the show, the audience and audience at home can vote for each couple 10 times per voting method (text, website, app). The score out of 30 from the judges and votes from America are totaled, and the couple with the lowest combined total is sent home.

Theme Weeks

Besides the first week, every week has a different theme that will help determine song choice, dances, and story telling. Some common themes every season include: Most Memorable Year, Disney Night, and Halloween Night. Other theme nights over the years have included: Rock & Roll Night, Broadway Night, Dedication Night, and nights centered around a specific artist like Michael Buble and Whitney Houston. There has also been some creative weeks over the seasons like Trio Week where a couple is assigned another pro to dance with them that week, and partner switch (The celebrities switch pros).
